games

For as long as I can remember computer games were my passion and not only from graphics/gameplay perspective but also from mechanics: how are they implemented and why in such way not the other.

I am not trying to close myself in genres but If I would have to decide then I would say without that the most fun I have playing Open World/Exploration, RPG, Creative/Sandbox games.
To point out a few of the titles I really hold dear. First two would be Planescape: Torment, Enderal: Forgotten Stories. Most probably the next on the list would be Gothic series(I think also nostalgia has something to say here), Witcher series or old Infinity Engine games like Baldur's Gate. But I think that's enough of those RPG's...
From Creative/Sandbox genres building games like Minecraft, Terraria, Starbound and... Skyrim... Maybe Bethesda games lack a lot in case of Story but they sure know how to make a living world waiting for you to explore and just before you notice you have sunk for another hours in exploring another dark dungeon with same generic enemies, with same generic loot, with same generic assets but hey it was fun.
I could just sit here and count the games I enjoyed and describe with a lot more details why I enjoyed them, but that is something that would require a whole blog just to cover that. So I will leave you with just a few more random yet amazing games: Stardew Valley, Graveyard Keeper, Omensight, Deponia, Hollow Knight, Darkest Dungeon, Hammerwatch, Doom, Mount & Blade, Darksiders series, Dragon Age, Heavy Bullets, Driftland, Torchlight II and many... many... many more.

And of course let's not forget about the board games! I enjoy them too but mostly cooperation based ones or Terraforming Mars or Catan. Beside those party games like Time's up!, Red7... And I could throw a few more in here too. And why no links? These are Borad Games that is a medium that requires a little more from it's players than computer games so I think You dear reader can manage to look them up. ;)
